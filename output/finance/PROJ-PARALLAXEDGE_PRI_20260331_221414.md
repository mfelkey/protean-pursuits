### 2. RECOMMENDED PRICING MODEL

**Tiered Subscription Model**

*Why:* The tiered subscription model best fits Parallax because it allows for customization and flexibility, catering to different customer segments with varying needs and budgets. This structure can drive upselling as users grow their reliance on the platform or expand their analytics requirements.

### 5. COMPETITIVE POSITIONING

Competitors in the sports betting analytics space include platforms like Bet Labs, Sports Insights, and DataFox. Their pricing varies widely but typically ranges from $39/month for basic services to custom enterprise solutions that can cost up to several thousand dollars annually.

**Positioning:** Parallax aims to be competitively priced at the lower end of the market with its Starter tier while offering more value in terms of features and insights compared to competitors like Bet Labs. The Pro tier is positioned slightly higher but still within a reasonable range, focusing on providing better analytics than most mid-tier services.

### 8. PRICE SENSITIVITY ANALYSIS

- **20% Price Reduction Impact:** If prices are reduced by 20%, the conversion rate could increase by approximately 15%. This would result in a higher number of paid users but lower MRR per user.
- **Unprofitable Floor:** At a monthly price point below $39 for the Pro tier, it becomes increasingly difficult to maintain profitability given the estimated COGS and CAC.

### 9. PRICING RISKS

1. **Commoditisation Risk** — To mitigate this risk, Parallax can continue to innovate by adding new features and data sources that competitors cannot easily replicate.
2. **Race-to-Bottom Price Wars** — Building strong brand loyalty through excellent customer service and unique value propositions can help avoid getting into a pricing war with competitors.
3. **Enterprise Procurement Friction** — Engaging in direct sales efforts to address enterprise clients' specific needs, such as custom SLAs and integration support, can reduce this risk.
