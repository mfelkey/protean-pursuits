## 1. Executive Summary

ParallaxEdge is an AI-powered sports betting analytics platform that gives bettors at every level access to the same quality of analytical intelligence previously available only to institutional players and professional analysts. The platform surfaces fair-value odds, model-driven edge calculations, and real-time market intelligence — so users decide with data, not instinct.

The name derives from the astronomical measurement technique: the gap between apparent and actual position. ParallaxEdge exists to show bettors exactly where the truth diverges from the market price.

### 1.1 The Core Proposition

Every legal sportsbook operates with a built-in margin — the vig — that tilts expected value against the bettor over time. The only durable path to positive expected returns is identifying and acting on mispricing before the market corrects. Professional syndicates and quantitative shops have done this for years with proprietary models, real-time data infrastructure, and closing line value (CLV) frameworks. Recreational and serious-hobbyist bettors have had none of it.

ParallaxEdge closes that gap.

## 2. Market Opportunity

### 2.1 Legal Sports Betting in the United States

The US sports betting market has undergone a structural transformation since the Supreme Court's 2018 _Murphy v. NCAA_ ruling overturned the federal prohibition on state-level sports wagering. What began as a Nevada-only market is now legal in 38 states and Washington D.C., with more than $600 billion in cumulative legal wagers placed since PASPA was repealed.

The scale of the market today is substantial and still growing. According to the American Gaming Association, US sports betting revenue reached $16.96 billion in 2025 — a 22.8% increase year-over-year — on total handle of $166.94 billion, itself up 11% from 2024. The average sportsbook hold rate reached 9.7% in 2025, the highest on record, reflecting both the industry's increasing sophistication and bettors' continued willingness to wager despite structural disadvantage. Tax contributions from licensed sportsbooks exceeded $3.7 billion in 2025, up 32.4% year-over-year.

Mobile has become the dominant channel. Approximately 84% of bets are now placed via mobile apps, collapsing the friction between impulse and wager and expanding the total addressable bettor population to anyone with a smartphone in a legal state. North America is also seeing the fastest growth in live betting globally, with a projected 15.4% CAGR through 2031 driven by continued state legalization and intensifying competition among major operators.

The market has not, however, democratized the analytical tools available to bettors. The sportsbook operators themselves employ sophisticated quantitative risk models; the bettors they serve largely do not. This asymmetry is the structural gap ParallaxEdge is built to close. The information layer — analytics platforms that sit above the wagering layer and are accessible from all 50 states without a gaming license — remains underbuilt relative to the scale and engagement of the wagering market itself.

### 2.2 The FIFA World Cup 2026 Catalyst

The FIFA World Cup 2026 is the single largest demand catalyst in ParallaxEdge's launch window. The tournament, co-hosted across the United States, Canada, and Mexico, expands to 48 teams and 104 matches for the first time in its history — up from 32 teams and 64 matches in 2022. Games will be played in US-friendly time zones, at iconic venues including MetLife Stadium in New York and Hard Rock Stadium in Miami, with kickoff scheduled for June 11, 2026.

The betting implications are significant. The 2022 World Cup generated more than $1.8 billion in US betting handle alone. Gabelli Funds projects that the 2026 edition will at least double that figure, citing the expanded match count, broader state legalization (38 states in 2026 vs. 31 at the end of 2022), and the tournament's North American hosting. Caesars Sports' head of soccer trading has described it as likely "the highest-handle soccer competition the industry has ever seen." The first-time bettor surge is equally notable: a Spotlight Sports Group survey of 600 sports fans found 70% plan to wager on the 2026 World Cup, yet only 7% feel confident doing so — a gap between intent and understanding that directly maps to the platform ParallaxEdge is building. A separate Paysafe survey found 60% of North and Latin American respondents planning to bet on the tournament.

For ParallaxEdge, the World Cup serves three simultaneous functions. It is a product launch event — the platform goes live with full soccer model coverage timed to group stage kickoff. It is a demand generation engine — a month of daily high-profile fixtures drives organic traffic, social amplification, and waitlist conversion. And it is a credibility-building mechanism — every model prediction published before kickoff and tracked publicly against results creates an auditable performance record at the moment of maximum global attention. There is no comparable event on the 2026 sporting calendar for generating this combination of reach, urgency, and analytical interest.

### 2.3 The Competitive Landscape

The sports analytics and betting intelligence market is a multi-layered ecosystem. ParallaxEdge occupies a specific and currently undercontested position within it.

**Odds Aggregators**
Platforms such as OddsChecker and basic odds comparison tools surface prices across books but provide no model output, no edge calculation, and no CLV framework. They are a starting point for line shopping, not a decision-support system. They are free, widely used, and represent the baseline that ParallaxEdge's free tier must exceed to justify registration.

**The Action Network**
The closest US analog to ParallaxEdge in the analytics and content space. Acquired by Better Collective for $240 million in 2021, Action Network generates approximately 70% of its revenue through sportsbook affiliate referrals and the remainder through subscriptions to its PRO tier. It offers odds comparison, line movement, bet tracking, expert picks, and proprietary analytical tools including BetSync for automated bet importing. It covers NFL, NBA, MLB, NCAA, NHL, and more — primarily US-focused. Its model, however, is editorial as much as quantitative: expert analysts, not transparent statistical frameworks with published methodologies and auditable track records. CLV is a secondary metric, not the primary frame. Soccer coverage is limited relative to its US sports depth. The Action Network's business model also creates a structural tension: affiliate revenue from sportsbooks and subscription revenue from bettors are not perfectly aligned interests.

**Handicapping and Tipster Services**
A large and fragmented category of pick-selling services, Discord communities, and subscription-based "sharps" offering selections rather than tools. Methodology is generally opaque, track records are selectively reported, and the model is fundamentally paternalistic — the service makes the call, the subscriber follows. ParallaxEdge is categorically different: it publishes its model, explains its methodology, and gives the bettor the tools to decide. This is not just a marketing distinction; it is a product philosophy enforced by the brand's standing commitment to never say "we predict" and to surface opportunities rather than sell picks.

**Raw Data Platforms**
FantasyLabs, Establish the Run, and similar services are primarily oriented toward daily fantasy sports, with betting applications as a secondary use case. They offer deep data but limited model integration and no CLV-centric performance framework.

**Institutional and Professional Tools**
Professional betting syndicates and quantitative shops operate proprietary infrastructure that is not commercially available at any price. This is the standard ParallaxEdge aspires to bring to the retail market — the "Bloomberg Terminal for bettors" framing — not a competitor in the conventional sense.

The market timing is favorable. The sports analytics software market was valued at approximately $2.3 billion in 2025 and is projected to reach $4.75 billion by 2030 at a 15.7% CAGR, with the predictive analytics tools segment growing fastest at 17.7% annually. The bettor-facing subset of this market — decision-support tools for individual bettors rather than team performance systems — is less developed and less saturated than its scale would suggest. ParallaxEdge is entering at the intersection of a maturing wagering market, a World Cup demand surge, and a structural gap in transparent, model-backed bettor intelligence.

## 3. Product & Technology

### 3.1 Platform Overview

ParallaxEdge is a web-first platform built in Next.js, delivered as both a browser application and a Progressive Web App (PWA). The platform covers three primary sports at launch — NFL, NBA, and soccer — across five competitions: NFL regular season and playoffs, NBA regular season and playoffs, English Premier League, FIFA World Cup 2026, and UEFA Champions League.

The product vision: **the Bloomberg Terminal for bettors**. Professional-grade tools, transparent AI-driven fair-value odds, and the data infrastructure to make genuinely informed decisions.

### 3.3 Core Features

**Odds Dashboard**
Real-time odds aggregation across all major US sportsbooks via The Odds API. Vig calculation and no-vig fair price displayed for every market. Free tier: 5 books. Sharp: full book set.

**Parallax Model — Fair Value Engine**
Proprietary AI models generate fair-value odds for every covered game. NFL model: EPA-based power ratings with rest, home field, and weather adjustments. NBA model: play-by-play based four-factor ratings with referee tendency scoring. Soccer model: Dixon-Coles xG framework across EPL, WC2026, and UCL fixtures. Each output includes win probability, fair-value odds for spread/moneyline/totals, edge percentage vs current market, confidence score, and a two-tier data quality gate (outputs below 0.70 DQ score are suppressed entirely; 0.70–0.85 carry a visible flag).

**Line Movement Intelligence**
Real-time line movement charted from open to current for all covered games. Pinnacle (the sharpest closing-line book globally) highlighted distinctly. Sharp tier only.

**Bet Tracker with CLV**
Manual bet logging with automatic result population and closing-line value calculation. Free: 50 bet limit. Sharp: unlimited. Performance dashboard with win rate, ROI, P&L, and average CLV — the key metric for assessing whether a bettor is finding genuine edge.

**Alerts Engine**
Push and email alerts for line moves (2+ point spread, 3+ total) and model edge thresholds. Delivered within 2 minutes of trigger via Firebase FCM. Sharp tier only.

**Public Track Record**
Every model prediction published before game time, permanently logged with model probability, market odds at publication, closing odds, CLV, and result. No auth required to view. This is the platform's primary trust-building asset.

### 3.5 Data Science Methodology

The platform's model outputs are built on transparent, published statistical methodologies:

- **Soccer:** Dixon-Coles Poisson model with xG inputs from Understat (EPL), StatsBomb Open Data (WC2026), and a to-be-confirmed UCL source
- **NFL:** EPA-based power ratings from nflfastR/nflreadr historical data with real-time injury and weather integration
- **NBA:** Four-factor ratings from BALLDONTLIE historical data with referee foul-rate adjustment

Model performance targets: Brier Score below 0.240 (NFL), 0.230 (NBA), 0.220 (soccer). Positive CLV on more than 55% of flagged value bets. All targets measured via walk-forward validation.

Development follows strict Test-Driven Development (TDD) with minimum coverage requirements across all components. No feature ships without passing acceptance criteria.

The World Cup is both a product launch and a demand generation event. The platform's public track record — model predictions published 24 hours before every covered WC2026 fixture — functions as a live product demonstration to an audience in peak engagement.

### 4.2 Launch Campaign: "The Edge Is Live"

The launch campaign leads with proof, not claims. During the World Cup group stage, ParallaxEdge publishes AI model outputs for every covered fixture before kickoff and tracks all results publicly. The campaign IS the product demo.

**Week-by-week pre-launch cadence (6 weeks before go-live):**

- **Weeks 6–5:** Model signal cards for top fixtures published on X and Instagram. No product mention. Voice and methodology established.
- **Weeks 4–3:** Brand name introduced. Platform teased. Waitlist CTA activated.
- **Weeks 2–1:** Full launch campaign. Influencer seeding. Final waitlist push.

**Launch week activation:**

- Day 1: 60-second brand film released across all channels
- Day 3: First public model signal card published (opening WC2026 match)
- Day 5: Early access seeded to sports data and analytics creators
- Day 7: Platform opens to waitlist; first 1,000 users receive Sharp tier free for the group stage

### 4.3 Promotional Strategy — World Cup Group Stage

The first 1,000 sign-ups receive Sharp access free during the group stage (approximately one billing cycle). A 35% promo-to-paid conversion rate is assumed, yielding 350 paid Sharp subscribers from the promo cohort at the group stage conclusion. This cohort provides the earliest proof-of-product data and seeds word-of-mouth among the most engaged early adopters.

### 4.4 Social Media Strategy

Social media is the primary acquisition and credibility channel for ParallaxEdge, particularly in the pre-revenue period before the product is live. The strategy is built around demonstrating the model in public before asking anyone to pay for it. Each platform has a distinct role, audience, and content format. All channels apply the teal/amber signal system and the brand's direct, data-literate voice consistently.

**Content split (70/20/10) — applies across all platforms:**

- **70% Signal posts:** Pre-match AI model outputs, odds movement alerts, edge breakdowns, and real-time market intelligence formatted as clean signal cards
- **20% Education:** Model methodology explainers, expected value threads, "how to read a confidence interval," CLV explainers — content that builds trust with analytically curious users and onboards newcomers
- **10% Culture:** World Cup moments, reactive in-game commentary backed by data, community wins, and human voice on top of the data backbone

---

**X (Twitter) — Primary Channel**

Role: Real-time intelligence distribution and community building. The highest-frequency platform and the primary driver of organic follower growth in the sports analytics space.

Content: Signal cards published before every covered fixture — model edge, confidence score, and key factors displayed in the brand's teal/amber visual system. Odds movement alerts when Pinnacle or sharp books move significantly. Reactive match commentary anchored in data, not emotion. Thread-format explainers on methodology and market dynamics.

Cadence: Multiple posts per match day across NFL, NBA, and soccer. Daily during the World Cup group stage.

Target: 10,000 followers within 6 months of launch. This is the primary follower metric in the Year 1 growth targets.

---

**Instagram — Visual Signal Cards and Explainers**

Role: Design-led signal distribution and brand building. Instagram's format rewards visual quality; ParallaxEdge's teal/amber signal card system is purpose-built for this environment.

Content: Designed pre-match signal cards for high-profile fixtures. Model explainer carousels — "how xG works," "what CLV tells you," "why Pinnacle is the sharpest closing-line book." World Cup visual content during the tournament. The aesthetic is sleek and data-forward: Bloomberg meets sport.

Cadence: Lower frequency than X — quality over volume. Major fixtures, significant line movement events, and educational series.

Note: Instagram is explicitly not a real-time channel. Signal cards published here are visual assets, not live intelligence. The gap between Instagram's format and X's speed is a feature, not a flaw — it keeps each platform's role distinct.

---

**YouTube Shorts — Short-Form Education**

Role: Discovery and education. YouTube Shorts' algorithmic distribution reaches sports and analytics audiences who are not yet following ParallaxEdge on other platforms.

Content: Short-form explainer videos (60–90 seconds): how the model calculates fair-value odds, what expected value means and why it matters, how to read a confidence interval, what closing line value measures and why it's the gold standard for betting performance. The visual language mirrors the platform's UI — showing the product in the context of explaining the concept.

Cadence: Regular cadence during the pre-launch build period and through the World Cup. Does not require match-day timing — evergreen content works for this format.

---

**Handle Strategy — Pre-Launch Priority**

All handles must be secured before the pre-launch social cadence begins (six weeks before go-live). Required platforms: X, Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn, YouTube, Discord. These are confirmed pre-launch checklist items. A consistent @parallaxedge handle across all platforms is the baseline; any variation creates brand fragmentation at the moment of maximum growth.

Discord is listed in the pre-launch checklist as a handle to secure. It is also the designated community platform for Pro tier subscribers (priority Discord access is a Pro tier feature). The Discord server is therefore both a brand asset and a product feature — it should be architected with that dual function in mind from day one.

---

**Influencer and Seeding Approach**

Seed to sports data creators, football analytics accounts, and quantitative sports journalism — not traditional tipster or picks channels. This is a deliberate positioning choice, not a budget constraint. Sports data and analytics creators have greater credibility with the target ParallaxEdge user, generate more durable engagement around methodology and edge rather than results, and carry significantly lower compliance and reputational risk than tipster-adjacent influencers.

Early access recipients receive the platform ahead of the public waitlist open. No scripted content — genuine reactions only. The product performing publicly is the campaign.

### 4.5 Affiliate Revenue

ParallaxEdge earns affiliate commissions by surfacing odds from partner sportsbooks within the platform. Affiliate links carry a visible "Affiliate partner" label and footer disclosure on all relevant pages in compliance with FTC guidelines and the platform's own transparency standards. Affiliate revenue is projected to begin in September 2026 (NFL launch) at $500/month, growing to $1,500/month by February 2027 as the user base and engagement scale.

A minimum of three sportsbook affiliate agreements must be executed before launch.

### 4.6 SEO & Content

The public track record page is the platform's primary SEO asset: a permanently growing, machine-updated table of predictions, results, and CLV outcomes for every covered game. This page requires no auth to view, generates long-tail search traffic organically, and is a differentiated trust asset versus tipster-model competitors.

### 6.2 Regulatory Position

**ParallaxEdge is not a sportsbook, gambling operator, or pick-selling service.** The platform provides analytics, model outputs, and decision-support tools. It does not accept wagers, hold user funds, or guarantee outcomes of any kind. This classification is material:

- No state gaming license is required for an analytics platform that does not accept wagers
- Affiliate relationships with licensed sportsbooks are governed by the sportsbooks' affiliate programs and applicable FTC affiliate disclosure requirements — not gambling-specific licensing
- The platform's responsible gambling framework (explicit "tool, not tipster" framing, age verification, self-exclusion support) is a product and ethical commitment, not a regulatory requirement at the analytics layer

This distinction should be reviewed by legal counsel as the platform scales into new states and markets.

### 6.3 International Trademark Strategy

The USPTO trademark application for "Parallax" / "ParallaxEdge" is budgeted and scheduled for filing before public launch. US registration establishes domestic rights and, critically, starts the clock on international filing options.

**Paris Convention Priority Window**

Under the Paris Convention, a USPTO filing date can be claimed as the priority date for a corresponding EUIPO application, provided that application is filed within six months of the US filing. This means ParallaxEdge does not need to file internationally at launch — but the six-month window after the USPTO application is submitted is the decision point for EU and UK protection. Missing that window does not foreclose international registration, but it surrenders the priority date advantage and exposes the mark to third-party filings in the interim.

**European Union Trademark (EUTM)**

A EUTM filed with the European Union Intellectual Property Office (EUIPO) provides protection across all 27 EU member states through a single application. This is directly relevant to ParallaxEdge given: EPL and UEFA Champions League coverage attracting UK and European users from launch; GDPR compliance obligations already requiring EU-region data infrastructure; and the first-to-file nature of EU trademark law, under which prior commercial use confers no rights — whoever registers first holds the mark.

The base EUTM filing fee is €850 (electronic submission) for one class of goods and services, with €150 per additional class. This is not a material cost relative to the risk of a third party registering the mark in the EU during the growth period.

**United Kingdom**

Following Brexit, EU trademarks no longer extend to the UK. A separate UK Intellectual Property Office (UKIPO) filing is required for UK protection. Given EPL coverage as a launch sport and the UK's position as the world's most active sports betting market, UK trademark protection warrants consideration alongside the EUTM — not as a substitute for it.

## Appendix C: Glossary of Sports and Gaming Terms

---

**1X2**
A three-way betting market used primarily in soccer. Bettors choose between a home win (1), a draw (X), or an away win (2). Unlike spread or moneyline markets, the draw is a distinct outcome with its own odds rather than a push.

**Asian Handicap**
A spread-style soccer betting market that eliminates the draw by assigning a fractional or half-goal handicap to each team. Common lines include -0.5, -1.5, or -1.75. The half-goal increment ensures one side always wins, removing the three-way outcome structure of 1X2 markets and reducing the sportsbook's hold.

**BTTS (Both Teams to Score)**
A soccer betting market in which bettors wager on whether both teams will score at least one goal during the match, regardless of the final result. A popular alternative to straight result markets because it remains live until the final whistle independent of which team is winning.

**Brier Score**
A statistical measure of the accuracy of probabilistic predictions, calculated as the mean squared difference between predicted probabilities and actual outcomes. Lower is better. A Brier score of 0.25 is equivalent to random prediction on a binary outcome; a score of 0.00 represents perfect calibration. ParallaxEdge targets Brier scores below 0.240 (NFL), 0.230 (NBA), and 0.220 (soccer).

**Calibration**
The degree to which a model's predicted probabilities match observed outcome frequencies over a large sample. A well-calibrated model that assigns 60% probability to an outcome should be correct approximately 60% of the time when that probability is assigned. Calibration error is distinct from raw accuracy — a model can be accurate but poorly calibrated if it systematically over- or under-states confidence.

**CLV (Closing Line Value)**
The difference between the odds at which a bet was placed and the closing odds for the same market immediately before the game begins. Positive CLV means a bettor obtained better odds than the market's final price; negative CLV means the bettor got worse odds than the close. CLV is widely regarded as the most reliable indicator of long-term betting edge because closing lines represent the market's most informed, efficient pricing.

**Confidence Score**
A ParallaxEdge-specific metric (0.000–1.000) attached to each model output indicating the reliability of the prediction given data availability and historical model performance on similar fixtures. Outputs with confidence scores below 0.60 display a "Lower confidence — limited data" warning. Distinct from the probability estimate itself.

**Data Quality Score (DQ Score)**
A ParallaxEdge-specific gate metric (0.000–1.000) reflecting the completeness and freshness of the input data used to generate a model output. Outputs below 0.70 are suppressed entirely and never shown to users. Outputs between 0.70 and 0.85 are published with a visible data quality flag. Outputs above 0.85 publish normally.

**Dixon-Coles Model**
A Poisson regression model for soccer match outcome prediction, developed by Mark Dixon and Stuart Coles in 1997. It extends the basic Poisson model by correcting for the statistical dependency between low-scoring outcomes (0-0, 1-0, 0-1, 1-1), which occur more frequently than independent Poisson processes predict. The model uses historical goal-scoring rates — increasingly refined through expected goals (xG) inputs — to generate win/draw/loss probabilities and fair-value odds for all markets.

**EPA (Expected Points Added)**
An American football metric quantifying the value of each play in terms of its effect on a team's expected point total for the drive. EPA accounts for down, distance, field position, and game context, making it a more informative measure of offensive and defensive performance than raw yardage or scoring statistics. The ParallaxEdge NFL model is built on EPA-based team power ratings derived from nflfastR historical data.

**Expected Goals (xG)**
A soccer metric that quantifies the quality of a shot by assigning a probability (0 to 1) that the shot will result in a goal, based on factors including shot location, assist type, body part used, and game state. Summing xG across all shots in a match produces a team's expected goals total — a more reliable indicator of underlying performance than actual goals scored, which carry significant variance. xG is the primary input into the ParallaxEdge Dixon-Coles soccer model.

**Four-Factor Model**
An NBA analytical framework identifying the four statistics most correlated with winning: effective field goal percentage (eFG%), turnover rate, offensive rebounding percentage, and free throw rate. Developed by Dean Oliver. The ParallaxEdge NBA model uses four-factor ratings adjusted for pace, opponent strength, referee tendency, and rest to generate fair-value odds.

**Handle**
The total dollar amount wagered with a sportsbook over a given period or event. Handle is the top-line volume metric for the sports betting industry. It is distinct from revenue: a sportsbook with $1 billion in handle and a 9% hold retains approximately $90 million in gross gaming revenue.

**Hold (Vig / Juice)**
The sportsbook's built-in margin, expressed as the percentage of total handle retained as revenue after paying winning bettors. Also referred to as the vigorish (vig) or juice. A standard -110/-110 spread market carries approximately 4.5% hold. The no-vig or "fair" price removes this margin to show the implied probability each side would carry in an efficient market.

**Kelly Criterion**
A mathematical formula for determining the optimal fraction of a bankroll to wager on a bet given the estimated edge and the odds. Full Kelly maximizes long-term bankroll growth but produces high variance; fractional Kelly (typically 25–50% of the full Kelly stake) is more commonly used in practice to limit drawdown risk. The Kelly optimizer is a planned Elite tier feature.

**Line Movement**
The change in a betting line from its opening price to its current or closing price. Sharp line movement — particularly movement at Pinnacle or other sharp books before recreational books adjust — is widely interpreted as an indicator of professional (sharp) money entering the market. ParallaxEdge tracks and displays line movement history for all covered games, with Pinnacle highlighted distinctly as the primary sharp-book reference.

**Moneyline**
A bet on which team will win a game outright, expressed as American odds. A favorite is indicated by a negative number (e.g., -150, meaning a $150 wager wins $100); an underdog by a positive number (e.g., +130, meaning a $100 wager wins $130). No spread is involved — the result is win or lose.

**No-Vig Odds (Fair Odds)**
The implied odds after the sportsbook's margin (vig) has been mathematically removed. Converting both sides of a market to implied probabilities, normalizing them to sum to 100%, and converting back to odds produces the no-vig price — the "true" market probability in the absence of the house edge. ParallaxEdge displays no-vig fair odds alongside raw market odds for all covered games.

**Pinnacle**
A sportsbook widely regarded as the sharpest and most efficient closing-line book in the global sports betting market. Pinnacle accepts high limits, does not restrict winning bettors, and consequently attracts professional and institutional bettors whose action shapes its lines toward efficiency. Pinnacle's closing line is the de facto benchmark for CLV calculation. ParallaxEdge always displays Pinnacle odds regardless of affiliate status.

**Sharp / Sharp Bettor**
A bettor whose wagers are consistently informed, large in volume, and capable of moving lines at major sportsbooks. Sharp money refers to bets placed by this class of bettor. Sportsbooks monitor sharp action as a signal of market inefficiency and adjust lines accordingly. The ParallaxEdge Sharp tier is named for this concept — the tier for bettors who approach wagering analytically.

**Sharp Money / Steam Move**
A rapid, coordinated line movement triggered by significant professional or syndicate betting action. Steam moves typically cascade across multiple books as each adjusts to replicate the sharpest market price. The ability to detect and alert on steam moves is a planned Pro tier feature.

**Spread (Point Spread)**
A handicap applied to the favored team to level the betting market. A -3.5 spread means the favorite must win by 4 or more points for a spread bet on them to win; a +3.5 spread means the underdog can lose by up to 3 points and still cover. The standard market in NFL and NBA betting.

**Total (Over/Under)**
A bet on whether the combined score of both teams in a game will be over or under a line set by the sportsbook. Totals markets exist for full-game scores, first-half scores, and individual team scoring. The ParallaxEdge model generates fair-value totals lines for all covered sports.

**Walk-Forward Validation**
A model evaluation methodology in which the model is trained on historical data up to a point in time, tested on the immediately following period, and then retrained on the expanded dataset before testing on the next period. This approach mimics real-world deployment conditions — the model never sees future data during training — and produces performance estimates that are more reliable than standard cross-validation for time-series prediction problems. All ParallaxEdge models are validated using walk-forward methodology.
