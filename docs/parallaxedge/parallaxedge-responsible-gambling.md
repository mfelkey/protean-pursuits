## 4. Understanding Problem Gambling

Gambling is a form of entertainment. For most people it remains enjoyable and within their control. However, for some individuals gambling can become problematic, leading to financial, emotional, and social harm.

Problem gambling is characterised by a loss of control over gambling behaviour, continued gambling despite negative consequences, and increasing preoccupation with gambling activity.

**Signs that gambling may be becoming a problem include:**

- Spending more time or money on gambling than intended
- Gambling to recover previous losses ("chasing losses")
- Neglecting work, family, or other responsibilities due to gambling
- Borrowing money or selling possessions to fund gambling
- Feeling anxious, irritable, or distressed when not gambling
- Lying to friends or family about gambling activity
- Using gambling as a way to cope with stress, anxiety, or depression
- Unsuccessful attempts to reduce or stop gambling

If you recognise any of these signs in yourself or someone you know, we encourage you to seek support. Help is available — see Section 7 of this policy for a list of organisations that can provide free, confidential support.

## 6. How We Use Data Responsibly

ParallaxEdge may monitor usage patterns on the platform for indicators of potentially harmful behaviour. Where our systems identify patterns consistent with problem gambling — such as unusually frequent or extended use — we may take the following steps:

- Display a responsible gambling reminder within the platform

- Temporarily restrict account access pending a wellbeing check

We will always act with sensitivity and respect when raising concerns with users. We will never share individual user data with third parties for commercial purposes. See our Privacy Policy for full details of how we handle your data.
